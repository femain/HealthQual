Strategic Planning Process

Q1: Executive Management of Hospital conduct annual Strategic Planning Process for the Hospital to develop Strategic Goals for the Hospital. 1 2 3 4

Q2: Hospital Management develops a strategy document containing Strategic Objectives that are prioritised, achievable, time-bound, actualised and measured. 1 2 3 4

Q3: Strategic Goals are constantly evaluated amongst patients, Hospital staff, visitors and other stakeholders by comparing results with the original specified goals. 1 2 3 4

Q4: Hospital Management identifies potential Blind Spots in Strategy Development. 1 2 3 4

Q5: Hospital Management determines core competencies of Healthcare Professionals in developing Strategic Goals. 1 2 3 4

Q6: Hospital Management reviews Strategic Challenges , and Strategic Advantages of Hospital in developing Strategic Goals. 1 2 3 4

Q7: Hospital Management reviews relevant data and information pertaining to Hospital’s strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats in developing its Strategic Goals. 1 2 3 4

Q8: Hospital Management reviews major shifts in technology, markets, healthcare services , patient and stakeholder preferences, competition, the economy, and the regulatory environment in the Strategic Planning Process. 1 2 3 4

Q9: Hospital Management includes information of long-term sustainability , needed core competencies , projections of future performance and competitors’ future performance in its Strategic Planning Process. 1 2 3 4

Q10: Hospital Management collects and analyzes relevant information on the Hospital’s ability to execute the Strategic Plan in its Strategic Planning Process. 1 2 3 4
